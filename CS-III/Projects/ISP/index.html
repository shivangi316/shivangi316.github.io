About ShelterSafe:
  ShelterSafe aims to unite all weather tracking, and disaster navigating organizations into one; Built to combine multiple factors such as food availability, shelter capacity, distance, and more to guide users to the ideal shelter. Sheltersafe will rely on radio communications and user input to provide the most accurate information to civilians about the resources available. Sheltersafe will also need to gain access to user location inorder to pinpoint shelters within a 50 mile radius.
  ShelterSafe is a hub for shelter information. It provides capacity, location, usefulness, and a natural disaster tracker. There are two applications, one with administrative control allowing the Red Cross, UNICEF, and other natural disaster centers to update information regarding the hazards, and another for citizens to gather information regarding hazardous conditions and usability of shelters.
  ShelterSafe is an application that offers information on how to take safety measures in the event of a natural disaster. To do so, it displays a map, accrediting all safety shelters in a nearby area, as well as information about that shelter.
